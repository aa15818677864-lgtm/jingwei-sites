Trading with China and facing a dispute?

Typical China-Related Disputes

(Common scenarios for U.S. clients who need dispute resolution counsel in Mainland China)

  • A Chinese supplier or manufacturer failed to deliver on time, stopped shipping, refused rework or refund, or supplied goods that did not match the agreed specifications.
  • You have a product quality, warranty, return, or loss allocation dispute involving a factory, exporter, or trading company in Mainland China.
  • Your Chinese counterparty owes payment, keeps a deposit, withholds the balance, or refuses final settlement under a China-related contract.
  • A distribution, agency, OEM/ODM, procurement, or business cooperation agreement broke down and you need to assess remedies under China law.
  • The contract points to a China court or arbitration institution, or the evidence, goods, payment trail, or assets are located in Mainland China.
  • You need a lawyer in China to evaluate evidence, send a demand letter, negotiate, apply for preservation measures, file arbitration or litigation, or plan enforcement steps.
  • You need English-speaking legal support for a Mainland China dispute because the matter belongs in China, not with pure U.S. local counsel.
